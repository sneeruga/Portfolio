EXPLORE POSTERS

Overview

I used principles of combining type and image to create a poster for an upcoming Museum of Flight lecture. I considered how I could enhance the concept of exploration through color, composition and typography.

Timeline

1 Week

Role

Visual Designer

Type and Image

In the beginning, type and image were considered the same. As writing systems evolved, type and image became 2 separate systems. Typography/Writing represents a learned form of communication in which letters and words make up an language. Type is ultimately made of geometric letterforms, that combine to form words and sentences. Images represent the physical world and are a more visceral form of communication. Images are open to interpretation based on the viewer’s past experiences.

Poster 1: The Mystery of Space

For the first poster, I wanted to capture the enigma of outer space. Because space is so mysterious, there is an indubitable amount of courage needed to step out of your comfort zone and explore. This poster is designed to inspire museum-goers to be bold and unearth the secrets of the universe - pun intended.

Poster 2: Conquer Your Fears

For the second poster, I drew inspiration from the dark side of Star Wars – the empire, the sith, and vader, oh my! I designed this poster with one message in mind: Overcome your fears. As Yoda once said, fear is the path to the dark side.
