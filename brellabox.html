Overview

Brella-Box is a device that provides easy access information that most people in Seattle need to know before leaving the house: the temperature and the chance of rain. The device is Arduino-powered and is housed in a wooden laser-cut enclosure.

Our Process

Our team wanted to create a device that could indicate when we should pack our rain gear or not because of the ever changing weather conditions of Seattle. We created a concept called Brella-Bot, an arduino powered device that can indicate rain conditions and temperature. We began our process by having a team discussion of what kind of materials we would need to build this project and how we could achieve this in a 1.5 weeks. We sketched out many ideas and agreed to make a walnut box We tested three different number displays with several people and found that the most legible number display was a light rounded bar pattern. From our feedback, we reached a consensus on the number display and began the process of laser cutting the housing materials. Our prototype includes a temperature sensor and LED lights that indicate rain conditions and temperature. For our future iterations, we wish to create Brella-Bots with wireless capability and motion sensors.
